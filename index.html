Jonathan Schwarz

Welcome

I'm a Research Engineer at DeepMind, where I work with Yee Whye Teh, Razvan Pascanu and many others on problems in the intersection of probabilistic modelling and transfer/continual learning. A central question of my research is how to impose meaningful prior information on statistical methods. I'm also interested in approximate inference and Bayesian optimisation.

Bio

Previously, I completed my Master's degree in Artificial Intelligence from the University of Edinburgh (Top of the class, 2016) during which I worked with Taku Komura on deep generative models for semi-supervised learning and sequential data modelling.

Before focusing more deeply on Machine Learning, I conducted research in robotics, fascinated by progress in autonomous driving. My research focused on probabilistic path planning for mobile robots at the Fraunhofer IPA in Stuttgart, Germany.

During my undergraduate Studies in Computer Engineering (First, 2015), I also visited Takashi Nakajima's Lab near Tokyo, Japan where I applied computer vision methods to climate research, enabling the automated analysis of satellite data.
